## Sales and Client Messaging Rules

When writing client-facing replies, use this structure when appropriate:

1. Greeting based on time of day.
2. Address the client by name when available.
3. Introduce as Maksim from the Uplift team.
4. Thank them for their inquiry.
5. Ask for missing details only when needed.
6. Give a clear next step.
7. Offer a specific appointment time when possible instead of asking "what works for you?"

Example tone:

> Good afternoon, [Name]. Thank you for your inquiry - my name is Maksim from the Uplift team. I would love to assist you.

Pricing rules:

- Do not overquote exact tattoo pricing before design/stencil review.
- Use starting prices and ranges when needed.
- Explain that final pricing depends on size, placement, detail, color, and artist.
- Custom pricing is usually finalized after the artist creates or reviews the design.
- Flash tattoos often start around $199+.
- Custom tattoos start around $299+, but many meaningful custom projects are typically $500+.
- Lettering tattoos often start around $299+.
- Premium placements such as finger, hand, rib, neck, or foot may start higher.
- Booking deposits are non-refundable and applied to the total.
- Deposits can be transferred one time with proper notice.

Booking rules:

- Walk-ins are welcome daily from 11 AM to 7 PM.
- For scheduled appointments, encourage booking ahead.
- For rescheduling, request advance notice.
- Designs are typically reviewed during the appointment, not sent in advance.
- The client should bring references, placement ideas, sizing ideas, and preferred style notes.

## Schema Guidelines

Use structured data carefully. Do not overstuff schema.

Recommended schema types:

- LocalBusiness or TattooParlor for homepage/local pages
- Service schema for individual services
- FAQPage schema for FAQ sections
- Person schema for artist pages
- BreadcrumbList schema for important SEO pages
- OfferCatalog only when it makes sense
- ImageObject when useful for important portfolio images

Avoid irrelevant schema types. Do not use restaurant-specific schema such as `servesCuisine`.

Schema should be valid JSON-LD and inserted in Squarespace header/code injection only when appropriate.

Example LocalBusiness baseline:

```html
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "TattooParlor",
  "name": "UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing",
  "url": "https://uplifttattoo.com",
  "image": "https://uplifttattoo.com/path-to-image.jpg",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "497 Broome St",
    "addressLocality": "New York",
    "addressRegion": "NY",
    "postalCode": "10013",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "areaServed": [
    "SoHo",
    "New York City",
    "Manhattan",
    "Brooklyn"
  ],
  "openingHours": "Mo-Su 11:00-19:00",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.instagram.com/uplifttattoo",
    "https://www.instagram.com/upliftpiercing"
  ]
}
</script>
```

Always update image URLs, live page URLs, and service names before publishing.
