What makes fineline different - and why it matters.

Fineline tattooing is a discipline that requires a different skill set than traditional tattoo work. Thin lines have less margin for error. Delicate shading demands consistency. And the healed result depends entirely on how cleanly the ink was applied in the first place.

At UPLIFT, fineline is not an afterthought - it is the core of what we do. Our artists have built their practice around it. Every piece starts with a stencil review, every line is placed with intention, and every client leaves with clear aftercare instructions to protect the work.

The result is a tattoo that looks refined now and continues to look that way for years. That is what fineline should be.

How Long Do They Last

Fineline tattoos done right hold for years.

The longevity of a fineline tattoo depends on three things: technique, placement, and aftercare. When all three are handled correctly, a fineline piece stays clean and legible for a very long time.

  • Placement on low-friction areas (wrist, forearm, collarbone, ribcage) holds detail best
  • Sun exposure is the biggest enemy - SPF on healed tattoos extends sharpness significantly
  • Fingers, hands, feet, and high-friction areas fade faster and may need touch-ups
  • Proper healing in the first 2-4 weeks protects the ink from the surface down

What is a fineline tattoo?

A fineline tattoo uses a single needle or small grouping to create thin, precise lines and delicate shading. The result is a clean, minimal design with a refined look. At UPLIFT in SoHo, fineline is our primary specialty.

How long do fineline tattoos last?

With proper aftercare and sun protection, fineline tattoos stay sharp for many years. Placement matters most - low-friction areas like the forearm, collarbone, and ribcage hold detail best. Our artists apply fineline work with healed longevity in mind.

How much do fineline tattoos cost in NYC?

At UPLIFT, fineline flash tattoos start at $199. Custom fineline work starts at $299, and most meaningful pieces run $500 or more depending on size, detail, and placement. Final pricing is confirmed after your artist reviews the stencil on your appointment day.

Do fineline tattoos hurt more than regular tattoos?

Not necessarily. Single-needle work often feels lighter than traditional multi-needle machines. Pain depends primarily on placement, not the style. Most clients find fineline work very manageable - especially for smaller pieces and standard placements like the forearm or wrist.

Can I walk in for a fineline tattoo?

Yes. Walk-ins are welcome daily from 11 AM to 7 PM at 497 Broome St in SoHo. Flash fineline designs are available same-day. Custom fineline work may require a booked appointment depending on artist availability that day.

Will my fineline tattoo fade?

All tattoos soften slightly over time. Fineline tattoos are more susceptible to fading if placed in high-friction areas or exposed to excessive sun without protection. UPLIFT artists help you choose placement that maximizes longevity, and our aftercare instructions are designed to protect the work through healing.
