What Actually Affects the Price of a Tattoo

Five factors determine what your tattoo costs:

  • Size - Larger takes more time. More time costs more. This is the most straightforward factor.
  • Detail and complexity - A delicate fineline design with fine shading, botanical detail, or intricate patterns takes more skill and time than a simple outline.
  • Placement - Certain areas of the body are more technically difficult to tattoo - curves, ribs, hands, fingers. These placements start at a higher base.
  • Artist experience - More experienced artists command higher rates. Their work also tends to age better, heal cleaner, and look more refined. It's worth it.
  • Color - Black and grey fineline is standard. Color work (even light washes) adds time and cost.

Flash vs. Custom - What's the Right Choice for a First Tattoo?

Flash tattoos are pre-designed pieces that are priced clearly and available for same-day appointments. They're ideal for first-timers because there's no design prep required - you choose a design you love, it gets placed, and you get tattooed. The process is simple and fast.

Custom tattoos allow you to bring a personal concept - a specific symbol, a date, something drawn from your own life - and have an artist create a unique design around it. These take more preparation and cost more, but the result is something made specifically for you.

Neither is better than the other. It depends entirely on what you want.

Is a Cheap Tattoo Worth It?

This is one of the most important things to understand before your first tattoo. Tattoo pricing is directly tied to artist skill, studio quality, and the time required to do the work properly.

A very low price is usually a warning sign - not a deal. You'll wear your tattoo for the rest of your life. The savings from choosing the cheapest option available rarely outweigh the difference in quality, safety, and longevity.
