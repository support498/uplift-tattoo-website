What to Know Before Your First Tattoo in NYC

By UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing  |  SoHo, New York City

Your first tattoo in NYC should be a good experience - not a stressful one. The most important things to know: eat a full meal before your appointment, choose placement and size that fits your lifestyle, and book with an artist whose style matches what you want. The rest is easier than most people expect.

Getting your first tattoo is a milestone. It can also feel overwhelming if you don't know where to start - especially in a city like New York, where there are hundreds of studios and just as many opinions about what to do and where to go.

At UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing in SoHo, we work with first-time clients every single day. Here's everything we tell them before they come in.

Step 1 - Decide on Your Design

You don't need a fully formed idea before you walk in. But having a direction helps. Ask yourself:

  • Do you want something personal and custom, or are you open to a pre-designed flash piece?
  • What style appeals to you - fineline, minimalist, botanical, lettering, abstract?
  • Do you have reference images saved? Even loose inspiration helps your artist understand your aesthetic.

Flash tattoos are a great first tattoo option. The design is already drawn, the pricing is set, and the process is fast. Many of our most popular first tattoos are flash pieces.

For something custom, bring references to your appointment - photos, sketches, mood board screenshots. The more specific you can be, the more your artist can work with.

Step 2 - Choose Your Placement

Placement affects both how the tattoo looks and how much it hurts. For first-timers, these areas are popular because they're accessible, heal well, and have manageable sensitivity:

  • Inner forearm
  • Outer upper arm
  • Collarbone area
  • Upper back and shoulder blade
  • Ankle and lower leg
  • Behind the ear (small designs)

Areas that tend to be more sensitive for first-timers: ribs, spine, inner bicep, feet, and hands. These placements can absolutely work for a first tattoo - just know what you're signing up for.

Step 3 - What to Do the Day Before and Morning Of

  • Get a full night of sleep - your body heals better and handles sensation better when rested
  • Eat a complete meal 1-2 hours before your appointment - low blood sugar makes the experience harder
  • Stay hydrated - drink water the day before and morning of
  • Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours - it thins the blood and affects healing
  • Moisturize the skin in the area being tattooed for a few days leading up to the appointment
  • Wear clothing that makes the area easy to access - tank tops for arm/shoulder work, loose pants for leg work
  • Skip numbing cream unless your artist recommends it - most fineline work doesn't require it

What Happens During the Appointment

When you arrive, your artist will review your design idea or the flash you've selected. For custom work, they'll sketch or prepare a stencil. For flash, they'll size and place the stencil directly on your skin so you can see the placement before any ink touches you.

You'll have a chance to adjust placement and size before anything is finalized. Don't rush this step - take your time getting it right.

Once the stencil is positioned and you're happy with it, the tattooing begins. Most small fineline tattoos take 30 minutes to an hour. You can take breaks if needed.

Does a First Tattoo Hurt?

Most clients are surprised by how manageable it is - especially for fineline work. The most common description is a light scratching or burning sensation. It's not comfortable, but the vast majority of people find it very tolerable, especially for smaller pieces.

The areas most likely to cause discomfort: directly over bone (spine, ribs, collarbone), inner wrist, and anywhere with thin skin. Fleshier areas - outer arm, thigh, shoulder - tend to feel much easier.

Breathing helps. Tensing up makes it worse. Most people find a rhythm quickly and relax into it.

Both work for first-time clients. Walk-ins at UPLIFT are welcome daily from 11 AM to 7 PM. For flash designs, walk-ins are ideal - you can browse available designs, choose one you love, and get tattooed the same day.

For custom work or if you want a specific artist, booking in advance is the better approach. This gives your artist time to prepare your design and ensures the appointment isn't rushed.

Aftercare for Your First Tattoo

Your artist will go over aftercare before you leave. The basics:

  • Keep the tattoo covered for the first few hours
  • Wash gently with unscented soap, pat dry
  • Apply a thin layer of unscented moisturizer 2-3 times a day
  • Do not pick or scratch the tattoo as it peels
  • Keep it out of direct sunlight until fully healed (2-4 weeks)
  • Avoid swimming, soaking, or submerging in water during healing
