Behind the Ear Tattoo Design Ideas

  • A tiny crescent moon - One of the most popular. A small crescent sits naturally in the curved space behind the ear.
  • A small star or star cluster - One or three tiny stars in a minimal arrangement.
  • A minimal botanical - A single small leaf, a tiny fern frond, or a sprig at 1 inch or under.
  • A small flower - A tiny daisy or single blossom. Best at 0.5 to 1 inch in single-needle fineline.
  • A tiny butterfly - Wings symmetrically placed behind the ear with the body at the top.
  • A single word - One meaningful word in fine vertical lettering behind the ear.
  • A small snake - A coiled miniature snake in a tight composition.
  • A tiny wave or line mark - A single minimal mark or symbol that reads as a personal detail.

Does a Behind the Ear Tattoo Hurt?

Behind the ear is one of the more sensitive placements. The skin is thin, the bone is close to the surface, and some clients notice the sound of the machine more vividly due to bone conduction through the skull. Most clients describe it as more intense than the forearm - but because the designs are very small, the session is typically over in minutes, making the overall experience very manageable.

Behind the Ear Tattoo Aftercare

The most important aftercare consideration for this placement is keeping hair away from the healing tattoo:

  • Keep hair pinned or tied back during the healing period
  • Avoid hair products (sprays, dry shampoo, oils) near the area until fully healed
  • Avoid sleeping with hair rubbing directly on the tattoo
  • Wash gently with unscented soap as with any tattoo
  • Do not rub or irritate the area with earrings or headphones during healing

Frequently Asked Questions

Do behind the ear tattoos hurt?

Higher sensitivity than most placements - thin skin, close to bone, and some bone conduction of sound. Manageable because the designs are very small and sessions are brief. More intense than the forearm for most clients.

What designs work best behind the ear?

Micro designs work best - crescent moons, small stars, minimal botanicals, tiny flowers, butterflies, a single word, or a small symbol. The design needs to read clearly at under 1 inch in fineline.

How long does a behind the ear tattoo take to heal?

2 to 3 weeks for surface healing. Keep hair products away from the area and avoid hair rubbing against the fresh tattoo during the healing period.
