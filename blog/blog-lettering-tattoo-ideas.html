Lettering Tattoo Ideas That Work

  • A name - A parent, a child, a partner, someone significant. The most personal form of lettering tattoo.
  • A date - A birthday, an anniversary, a day that changed something. Roman numerals or standard numerals in clean formatting.
  • Coordinates - A specific location rendered in precise numerical lettering.
  • A single word - One word that carries weight. Love, free, enough, home, ever. The word does the work.
  • A short phrase - Two to five words from something meaningful - a lyric, a quote, a memory.
  • Initials - One or two letters in a refined style. Simple and subtle.
  • Handwriting - A message written in someone's actual handwriting, transferred and tattooed. Deeply personal.

Font Styles That Age Well

Not all font styles hold up equally over time as a tattoo. The best choices:

  • Clean serif - Classic, readable, and ages well. Works at most sizes.
  • Refined script - Elegant when done correctly. Requires adequate letter spacing to stay readable as the skin ages.
  • Simple sans-serif - Modern and clean. Very legible long-term.
  • Handwriting style - Personal and distinctive. The organic quality makes slight settling less visible.

Styles that can be problematic: very ornate scripts with extremely fine connecting lines, novelty or heavily stylized display fonts, and anything with letterforms that rely on near-invisible thin strokes. Your artist will advise if a requested style needs adjusting for longevity.

Best Placements for Lettering Tattoos

  • Inner forearm - Most popular placement. Flat, readable, visible without effort.
  • Collarbone - Horizontal phrases and short words work beautifully here.
  • Ribcage - Longer phrases in vertical orientation. More intimate placement.
  • Upper back / spine - Vertical single words or short phrases running along the spine.
  • Wrist - Small words or initials.
  • Ankle - Small single words or short dates.

The Most Important Rule for Lettering Tattoos

Triple-check every word, name, date, and character before approving the stencil. Spelling errors in tattoos are permanent. Check the stencil against your reference. Check it again. This is the one area where extra time at the start of the appointment is always worth it.

At UPLIFT, the stencil is placed on your skin before any tattooing begins. You will have the chance to review it carefully, adjust placement, and confirm that everything is exactly right. Do not rush this step.

Frequently Asked Questions

What font styles work best for lettering tattoos?

Clean serif, refined script, and simple sans-serif styles age best. Heavily ornate or very thin decorative fonts can blur over time. Your artist will advise if adjustments are needed for longevity.

How much does a lettering tattoo cost?

Lettering tattoos at UPLIFT start around $299. Pricing varies based on word count, letter size, font style, and placement.

What is the best placement for a lettering tattoo?

Inner forearm, collarbone, ribcage, upper back, and wrist are the most popular. Flat areas with minimal skin movement hold lettering cleanest long-term.

What should I consider before getting a lettering tattoo?

Triple-check every character before approving the stencil. Choose a font that will stay readable as fine lines settle over years. Placement affects both legibility and longevity.
