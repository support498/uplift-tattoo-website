Classic Matching Tattoo Ideas for Best Friends

These designs have staying power because they're simple, personal, and timeless in fineline style:

Sun and Moon

Complementary symbols that represent two different but connected forces. One friend gets the sun, the other the moon.

Matching Florals

The same flower on each person, or complementary flowers - rose and peony, daisy and wildflower. Beautiful in fineline.

Coordinates

The location where you met, a city that means something to both of you, or a place you shared. Clean lettering, minimal.

Matching Words or Phrases

A single meaningful word, a date, or a short phrase. Handwriting-style lettering feels personal and unique.

Arrows

Two arrows pointing toward each other, or complementary arrow directions. A simple, clean symbol of alignment.

Stars or Constellations

A shared star sign, a meaningful constellation, or simply two matching minimalist stars. Delicate and easy to place.

Butterflies

Matching butterfly designs are one of our most popular requests. Two identical pieces, or mirrored variants.

Pinky Promise

A small delicate linework hand symbol. Simple, playful, and unmistakably about friendship.

Matching Tattoo Ideas That Aren't Identical

Some of the best matching tattoos are complementary rather than copies. Two halves of a whole feel more intentional and personal. Ideas:

  • Lock and key
  • Two halves of a heart (simple fineline, not the cliche version)
  • Day and night - a sun for one, crescent moon for the other
  • Yin and yang split between two people
  • Two birds in flight, positioned to face toward each other
  • First and last word of a meaningful quote, one word each
  • Puzzle pieces
  • Mountain and wave - one person gets each

Matching Tattoos for Groups of 3 or More

Group matching tattoos work best when the design scales - something that looks intentional on each individual but clearly belongs to a set. Good approaches for groups:

  • The same small symbol on each person (star, flower, letter, moon phase)
  • A series - each person gets one element of a larger theme (sun, moon, star, cloud)
  • Matching initials or a shared date
  • A line of text split across three or more people
  • The same flash design in slightly different placements

What to Think About Before Choosing a Matching Design

A few things worth discussing with your friend before you book:

  • Size - Smaller tends to age better for matching designs. It also gives more placement options.
  • Placement - Will you want it visible or more private? Matching inner wrists, ankles, or collarbones are popular choices.
  • Style - Fineline is the most requested for matching tattoos because it works for every aesthetic and every skin tone.
  • Meaning vs. design - The best matching tattoos have both - something that looks good and means something specific to you two.
  • Will you still love it in 20 years? - Classic over trendy always wins long-term.
