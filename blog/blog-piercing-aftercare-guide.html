Piercing Aftercare Guide

By UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing  |  SoHo, New York City

Piercing aftercare is straightforward: keep it clean, do not touch it with unwashed hands, and let it heal. The most common reason a piercing has complications is disruption - touching, rotating, or exposing it to harsh products. Patience and consistency are the two things that matter most in the healing process.

Every client receives aftercare instructions at UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing in SoHo. This guide covers the full process in detail - what to use, what to avoid, and what to expect as the piercing heals.

Daily Piercing Aftercare Routine

  • Clean twice daily with sterile saline solution (wound wash or piercing aftercare spray)
  • Spray or apply saline directly to the piercing and surrounding skin
  • Allow to air dry or pat dry gently with a clean paper towel - never cloth, which harbors bacteria
  • Keep unwashed hands away from the piercing at all times
  • Change pillowcases frequently during healing, or use a clean paper towel under the ear while sleeping

What to Avoid During Healing

  • Rotating the jewelry - This disrupts the healing fistula. Modern piercing technique does not require rotation.
  • Alcohol or hydrogen peroxide - Too harsh for a healing piercing. They damage healthy tissue and slow healing.
  • Swimming in pools, oceans, or lakes - Exposure to bacteria and chemicals during active healing increases infection risk.
  • Sleeping on the piercing - Pressure and friction slow healing and can cause irritation bumps. Use a travel pillow or fold a pillow to reduce pressure.
  • Touching with unwashed hands - The most common source of complications. Do not touch unless cleaning.
  • Changing jewelry early - Wait until the piercing is fully healed. Changing too soon disrupts the healing channel and can cause irritation or infection.
  • Hair products, makeup, and fragrances near the piercing - Keep these away during active healing.

Healing Timelines by Placement

  • Earlobe: 6 to 8 weeks
  • Helix, flat, forward helix: 6 to 12 months
  • Conch, tragus: 6 to 12 months
  • Daith, rook: 9 to 12 months or longer
  • Nostril: 4 to 6 months
  • Septum: 6 to 8 weeks (when done correctly)

The outside of a piercing can look healed before the inside tissue channel is fully formed. Cartilage piercings especially look fine on the surface long before they are ready for jewelry changes. Follow the timeline, not just the appearance.

Downsizing

Downsizing - replacing the initial longer post with a shorter one after swelling has subsided - is an important step that should not be skipped. The initial jewelry is intentionally longer to accommodate swelling. Once swelling is gone (typically 4 to 6 weeks after the piercing), a shorter post reduces snagging, irritation, and accidental bumping. Frequently Asked Questions

How do you take care of a new piercing?

Clean twice daily with sterile saline. Avoid touching with unwashed hands, rotating the jewelry, using harsh products, and swimming during active healing. Patience and consistency are what matter most.

How long does a piercing take to heal?

Lobe piercings heal in 6 to 8 weeks. Cartilage piercings take 6 to 12 months. Daith and rook can take a year or more. The outside heals before the inside channel is complete - follow the full timeline.

What should you avoid with a new piercing?

Rotating the jewelry, harsh products like alcohol or hydrogen peroxide, swimming, sleeping on the piercing, touching with unwashed hands, and changing jewelry before it is fully healed.
