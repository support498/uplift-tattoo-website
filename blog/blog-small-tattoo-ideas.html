Best Placements for Small Tattoos

  • Inner wrist - Visible and personal. Works well for designs up to about 2 inches.
  • Ankle - Small designs tuck neatly at the ankle bone or wrap around it.
  • Behind the ear - Micro designs in this placement are subtle and refined.
  • Inner forearm - Readable and clean for most small designs.
  • Collarbone - A single small design sits naturally here, especially flowers or symbols.
  • Finger - Very small designs only. Note that finger tattoos fade faster than most placements.
  • Shoulder blade - Small designs read clearly on the flat upper back.

Do Small Tattoos Age Well?

In fineline, yes - when placed on stable areas and done by an artist who specializes in small-scale work. The common mistake is packing too much detail into a tiny design in a bold style. Lines that are too close together blur together as the skin naturally spreads over years.

Fineline technique - thin lines with adequate spacing - holds up significantly better at small scale. A simple, well-executed design at 1.5 inches will still look sharp 20 years later. A complex compressed design at the same size often won't.
