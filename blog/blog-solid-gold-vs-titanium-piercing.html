What Is Implant-Grade Titanium?

Not all titanium is the same. Implant-grade titanium (ASTM F136) is the specific alloy standard used in surgical and body implant applications. It is completely nickel-free, highly biocompatible, and one of the most inert materials you can put in your body.

Lower-quality titanium alloys exist - they may contain trace metals that can irritate sensitive tissue. At UPLIFT, all titanium jewelry meets implant-grade standards. We don't carry anything less.

What Does "Solid Gold" Mean - and Why Does It Matter?

Solid gold means the piece is made entirely from gold alloy - not gold plated over a base metal, not gold-filled. The difference matters significantly for piercings.

Gold-plated jewelry has a thin layer of gold over a cheaper base metal. Over time, the plating wears away, exposing the base metal to your skin and the piercing channel. This can cause reactions, irritation, and complications - especially in a fresh piercing.

All gold jewelry sold at UPLIFT is solid gold. Our pieces also carry a lifetime warranty - if the piece breaks or has a manufacturing defect, we stand behind it.

Which Is Better for a New Piercing?

Both are appropriate for initial piercings when they are implant-grade quality. Our piercers will recommend based on your specific anatomy, placement, and any known sensitivities.

For clients with nickel sensitivity or very reactive skin, titanium is often the safer first choice because it has essentially zero reactivity. For clients who want the most premium jewelry from day one and don't have known metal sensitivities, solid gold is a beautiful starting option.

Which Is Better for a Curated Ear?

For curated ear piercing and stacked looks, solid gold tends to dominate aesthetically. The warmth and weight of gold creates a richer, more intentional appearance when layering multiple pieces across a single ear.

That said, titanium's anodized color options - soft pinks, blues, purples - can add interesting visual variety to a curated ear build. Many clients mix both materials across different placements depending on the look they're going for.
