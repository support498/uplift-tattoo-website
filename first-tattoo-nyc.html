What to Expect

From idea to healed tattoo - here is the full picture.

01

The consultation

Your artist reviews your concept, placement, and sizing before creating a stencil. Most clients bring reference images. You do not need to arrive with a finished design.

02

The stencil placement

A stencil is placed on your skin so you can see the exact size and position before any needles are used. Adjustments are easy at this stage.

03

The tattoo session

Most small fineline pieces take 30 minutes to 2 hours. The sensation is typically a light scratching feeling. Most first-time clients are surprised by how manageable it is.

04

Wrap and aftercare

Your tattoo is wrapped before you leave. We walk you through aftercare in detail - washing, moisturizing, and what to avoid during healing.

05

The healing phase

Most fineline tattoos heal within 2-4 weeks. Avoid sun, soaking, and picking. The surface heals first, with deeper layers following over 4-6 weeks.

06

The final result

Once healed, fineline tattoos have a soft, clean look. Touch-ups are rare when proper aftercare is followed. We are always available for questions during the healing process.

Placement Guide

Choosing the right placement for your first tattoo.

Placement affects both the experience and the longevity of your tattoo. For first-timers, we typically recommend areas that are easy to manage, heal well, and are simple to show or hide depending on your preference.

  • Wrist and inner arm - easy to see, heals cleanly
  • Ankle and outer calf - less sensitive, great for small designs
  • Upper arm or shoulder - comfortable and versatile
  • Behind the ear - delicate and minimal
  • Collarbone - elegant with fineline work

Avoid finger, hand, rib, neck, and foot placements for your very first piece. These areas require more care and experience to heal well.

Before You Arrive

How to prepare for your first tattoo appointment.

●

Eat before you come in

Have a full meal 1-2 hours before your appointment. Low blood sugar can make you lightheaded during or after the session. Staying hydrated also helps.

●

Wear accessible clothing

Wear or bring clothing that gives easy access to the area being tattooed. Avoid tight sleeves, high collars, or layers that are difficult to move around.

●

Bring your references

Save 3-5 reference images to your phone - examples of styles, designs, sizing, and placement you like. You do not need a final design, just a direction.

●

Avoid alcohol

Do not drink alcohol the night before or the morning of your appointment. Alcohol thins the blood and can interfere with the tattooing process and healing.

First Tattoo FAQ

Questions first-time clients ask us most.

Does a first tattoo hurt?

Pain varies by placement and individual tolerance. Most clients describe fineline tattoos as a mild scratching or stinging sensation - manageable and much less intense than expected. Bony or thin-skinned areas such as ribs, fingers, and the spine tend to feel more intense. Our artists guide first-time clients toward comfortable placements when requested.

How much does a first tattoo cost in NYC?

At UPLIFT, flash tattoos start around $199+ and custom tattoos start around $299+. Many meaningful custom fineline pieces are typically $500 or more. Final pricing is confirmed after your artist reviews the design, size, and placement at your appointment. A $100 deposit is required to book.

Do I need an appointment or can I walk in?

Walk-ins are welcome daily from 11 AM to 7 PM at our SoHo studio at 497 Broome St. For a specific artist, custom design, or preferred time slot, booking in advance is recommended.

What should I bring to my first tattoo appointment?

Bring your photo ID, 3-5 reference images saved to your phone, a sense of the placement and size you want, and wear clothing that allows easy access to the area being tattooed. Eat a full meal beforehand and stay hydrated.

What tattoo style is best for a first tattoo?

Fineline and minimalist tattoos are the most popular choice for first-time clients. They heal cleanly, photograph beautifully, and are typically completed in a single session. Botanical, butterfly, script, and celestial designs are among the most requested first tattoo styles at UPLIFT.

How long does the healing process take?

Most fineline tattoos heal on the surface within 2-4 weeks. Deeper tissue healing takes 4-6 weeks. Follow aftercare instructions carefully - keep it clean, moisturized, out of direct sun, and avoid soaking in water until fully healed.

Can I bring someone with me?

Yes. You are welcome to bring a friend or partner. We ask that guests remain respectful of the studio environment and the artist's workspace during the session.
