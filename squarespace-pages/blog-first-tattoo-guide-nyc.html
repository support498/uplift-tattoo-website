Your Complete First Tattoo Guide for NYC

Everything you need to know before, during, and after your first tattoo - from a fineline studio in SoHo.

Before Your First Tattoo Appointment

Preparation makes the difference between a smooth session and an uncomfortable one. In the 24 hours before your appointment: eat a full meal, hydrate well, and avoid alcohol. Get a good night of sleep. Arrive wearing clothing that gives easy access to the placement area.

Choosing Your First Design

Smaller, simpler designs are the most common choice for first-timers - and for good reason. They require shorter sessions, heal more predictably, and give you a strong starting point before you commit to larger work. Fineline tattoos are especially popular: clean lines, minimal color, and a refined aesthetic that works on virtually any placement.

Placement Considerations

Some placements are more comfortable for first-timers than others. The outer arm, thigh, and calf are typically the least sensitive areas. Inner arm, ribs, sternum, and feet tend to be more intense. Your artist can advise on what feels appropriate given your chosen design and size.

What Happens During the Session

Your artist will prepare your skin, apply the stencil, and ask for your approval before starting. During the tattoo, most clients describe fineline work as a light scratching sensation. You can ask for breaks at any point - there is no pressure to push through discomfort.

Aftercare Basics

  • Keep the tattoo clean and moisturized for the first 2 weeks
  • Avoid direct sun exposure until fully healed
  • Do not soak in water (pools, baths, ocean) for at least 2 weeks
  • Do not pick or scratch as the tattoo peels
  • Your artist will provide specific aftercare instructions for your piece
