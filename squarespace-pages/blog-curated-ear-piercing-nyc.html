What Is a Curated Ear?

A curated ear is an intentional collection of piercings styled to work together as a complete look. Rather than adding piercings randomly over the years, a curated ear is planned - considering placement, jewelry style, metal, and how pieces will relate to each other visually.

A curated ear session at UPLIFT is a consultation plus same-day piercing. Your piercer will map out placements with you, recommend jewelry that works together, and walk you through the sequencing of which piercings to add and when based on healing timelines.

Common Placements for a Curated Ear

  • Lobe (standard) - the foundation of any ear
  • Second and third lobe - builds a stacked lobe look
  • Helix - outer cartilage rim, upper ear
  • Flat - inner cartilage, ideal for flat-back studs
  • Conch - inner ear, a bold center-stage placement
  • Tragus - small cartilage by the ear canal
  • Daith - inner fold, a distinctive cartilage option
  • Rook - upper antihelix, adds depth to the inner ear

How Many Piercings Can I Get in One Session?

Most people get 2-4 new piercings per session. Doing too many at once can extend healing time and make aftercare more complex. Your piercer will advise on what's realistic for your healing capacity and lifestyle.

Jewelry for a Curated Ear

The most elevated curated ears use consistent metal - all gold, or all gold with selective titanium accents. UPLIFT carries a range of solid gold flat-backs, studs, clickers, and hoops that are designed to work together. Gold jewelry starts at $99+ per piece. Titanium options start at $50+.
