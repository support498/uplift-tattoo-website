The Tattoo Process

What is a flash tattoo?

A flash tattoo is a pre-designed tattoo selected from an artist's existing collection. Flash designs at UPLIFT are available for walk-in appointments and same-day booking. They start at $199 and are a great option for clients who want a clean, finished design without a custom consultation.

Can I bring my own tattoo design?

Yes. You are welcome to bring reference images, sketches, or design ideas to your appointment. Our artists use client references to build custom stencils tailored to your size and placement. The final design is created and refined by the artist based on your input.

When will I see my design before the appointment?

Designs and stencils are finalized on the day of your appointment. This allows the artist to size and position the design directly on your body for the best result. We do not send designs digitally in advance for most projects. Bring your reference images and placement ideas when you arrive.

How long does a tattoo session take?

Session length depends on the design. Small flash tattoos typically take 30 to 60 minutes. Medium custom pieces may take 1 to 2 hours. Large or detailed projects are scheduled as full-day or multi-session appointments. Your artist will give you a time estimate during check-in.

Can you cover or rework an existing tattoo?

Coverups and reworks are available depending on the existing tattoo's size, darkness, and placement. Please send a photo of your existing tattoo when submitting your inquiry. Our artists will assess whether a coverup or laser preparation is recommended before booking.

Pain, Tipping & Etiquette

How much does it hurt to get a tattoo?

Pain level varies by placement. Bony areas - such as ribs, hands, spine, and feet - tend to be more sensitive. Fleshy areas like the upper arm, outer thigh, and forearm are generally more comfortable. Fine-line tattoos use thinner needles, which many clients find easier to tolerate than traditional or bold styles. First-time clients often find the experience more manageable than expected.

How should I prepare for my tattoo appointment?

Arrive well-rested and well-fed on the day of your appointment. Eat a full meal 1 to 2 hours before your session to stabilize your blood sugar. Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the tattoo placement. Avoid blood thinners such as aspirin unless medically prescribed.

How much should I tip my tattoo artist?

Tipping is not required but is appreciated. A standard tip for tattoo artists is 15 to 20 percent of the total service cost. Tips go directly to your artist and reflect your appreciation for their work and care during the session.

Tattoo Safety & Health

Can I get a tattoo if I am pregnant or breastfeeding?

We do not tattoo clients who are pregnant. If you are breastfeeding, we recommend consulting your doctor before booking. Your safety and your baby's health are the priority. We are happy to help you plan your appointment for when the time is right.

What conditions or medications may affect my tattoo appointment?

Certain conditions and medications can affect how skin takes ink or heals. These include blood thinners, recent use of Accutane (within 6 months), sunburned or irritated skin at the placement site, active skin rashes or infections, and certain autoimmune or bleeding conditions. Please let your artist know about any medications or health conditions during your consultation. We may reschedule if proceeding is not considered safe.

Is tattooing safe? What safety standards does UPLIFT follow?

UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing follows New York City health and safety regulations. We use single-use, sterile needles for every client, professional-grade ink, and clean sterile technique throughout every session. All workstations are sanitized between appointments. Our studio is licensed and inspected by the New York City Department of Health.

Piercing at UPLIFT

Do you offer walk-in piercings?

Yes. Walk-in piercings are available daily from 11 AM to 7 PM based on piercer availability. For the best same-day experience, arrive earlier in the day or contact the studio before visiting to confirm availability.

What types of piercings do you offer?

UPLIFT offers a full range of ear and body piercings, including: lobe, helix, flat, conch, tragus, rook, daith, orbital, forward helix, industrial, nose, nostril, septum, navel, and more. Our piercers will guide you on placement, healing timelines, and jewelry selection during your appointment.

Can I bring my own jewelry to UPLIFT for a new piercing?

No. All jewelry for new piercings must be purchased through the studio. This ensures proper gauge, grade, and material for safe healing. UPLIFT uses solid 14k gold and implant-grade titanium only. Outside jewelry does not meet the quality and sizing standards required for a fresh piercing.

How much does a piercing cost at UPLIFT?

Piercing pricing includes a service fee plus jewelry. Solid 14k gold jewelry starts at $99 and includes a lifetime warranty. Implant-grade titanium jewelry starts at $50. Final cost depends on jewelry selection and the piercing type. All pricing is reviewed before your appointment begins.

How long does a piercing take to heal?

Healing time varies by piercing type. Lobe piercings typically take 6 to 8 weeks. Cartilage piercings such as helix, conch, and rook can take 6 to 12 months. Body piercings vary widely. Proper aftercare - including daily saline rinses and avoiding unnecessary contact - significantly reduces healing time and the risk of irritation.

What is piercing aftercare at UPLIFT?

Your piercer will review aftercare instructions before you leave. The standard protocol is a sterile saline spray twice daily, no touching or rotating the jewelry, and no swimming in pools, oceans, or hot tubs until the piercing is fully healed. Avoid makeup or skincare products near the piercing site during healing.

What is the difference between a new piercing and a jewelry change?

A new piercing is performed on unpiercedtissue using a sterile needle and fresh jewelry. A jewelry change - also called a downsize, install, or removal - is a service performed on a healed or partially healed piercing. Complimentary check-ups are included. Downsizing, installs, and removals may carry a small service fee.

What is the age requirement for piercings?

You must be 18 years or older with a valid government-issued photo ID for all piercing services at UPLIFT Tattoo & Piercing. We do not pierce minors.
